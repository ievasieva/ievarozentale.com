How I Work

My work combines facilitation, behavioural insights, narrative approaches, and reflective practices.

These methods help groups explore complex problems, engage with uncertainty, and develop thoughtful responses.

Influences

What Shapes My Practice

My approach draws on several traditions:

Facilitation & Participatory Methods

Creating structured spaces where diverse perspectives can be explored. Good facilitation design ensures that every voice can contribute and that groups can move through complex terrain together.

Behavioural Insights

Understanding how human decision-making and group dynamics influence outcomes. Drawing on behavioural science helps design processes that work with how people actually think and behave, rather than how we assume they do.

Narrative Approaches

Exploring how organisations understand their challenges and possible futures. Narrative methods help surface the stories and assumptions that shape how we see problems and what we believe is possible.

Theory U & Reflective Practices

Supporting deeper listening and awareness in collective processes. Theory U offers a framework for understanding how groups can move from reactive to more generative and creative responses.

Futures Thinking

Exploring possible future developments and their implications for present decisions. Futures thinking helps organisations develop more adaptive and resilient strategies by expanding their view of what might be possible.

In practice

How These Come Together

In practice, I draw on these traditions fluidly depending on what a particular group or challenge needs. Some situations call for structured problem-solving; others require space for deeper reflection and dialogue.

My goal is always to create processes that are well-designed, genuinely engaging, and that help groups move forward with greater clarity and confidence.

The best facilitation is often invisible — participants are so engaged with the substance of their work that the process itself recedes into the background.
